Water Quality Prediction

EY Open Science AI & Data Challenge

Problem

Water quality is hard to monitor continuously; public models need strong geospatial and climate signal to generalize across regions.

Data

Geospatial grids plus climate layers; engineered Landsat and TerraClimate-derived features aligned to prediction targets.

Method

NFL Big Data Bowl 2026

Cross-Framework Model Benchmarking

Problem

Same architecture, two frameworks—where do accuracy, convergence, and wall-clock training actually diverge on real tracking-scale workloads?

Data

NFL Big Data Bowl 2026 large-scale player tracking sequences (frame-level trajectories and derived sequence tensors).

Method

Matched sequence deep nets in PyTorch vs TensorFlow; identical training recipes; tuned batching and GPU utilization.

Result

PyTorch reached roughly 25% lower RMSE with faster convergence; pipeline changes cut training time by 40%.

Insight

"Same model" is a myth at production scale—framework + dataloader + GPU path is part of the system; pick the stack for measurable RMSE and throughput, not vibes.

Aug – Nov 2024

Class Imbalance in Image Data

Problem

Imbalanced sports imagery breaks naive training—models chase head classes and collapse on rare events.

Data

Multi-class sports photos with skewed label frequencies; held-out validation for honest comparisons.

Method

ResNet50, GoogLeNet, VGG16, AlexNet + resampling variants: Gaussian Pyramid, Bicubic, SRCNN, Wavelet.

Result

Up to +33.7% val accuracy (AlexNet) and +22.0% (VGG16); ResNet50 83.8% → 86.8% validation accuracy with less overfitting.

Insight

Resampling is not neutral—shallow nets gained the most; deeper architectures already absorb imbalance, so marginal gains shrink as capacity rises.

Computer Vision ResNet50 Imbalanced Data
